Membrane Products - BWRO, SWRO, NF, UF, MBR

RO membrane elements are the heart of any reverse osmosis water treatment system. Genoasis supplies spiral-wound membrane elements in all standard configurations:

  • BWRO (Brackish Water RO): For feed water TDS up to 10,000 ppm at 10–20 bar operating pressure. The standard choice for groundwater and borewell treatment across India, the GCC, Africa, and Australia's brackish aquifer regions.
  • SWRO (Seawater RO): For seawater desalination at TDS up to 45,000 ppm, operating at 55–70 bar. Used at coastal desalination plants across the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Oman, Qatar, Australia (Perth, Sydney), and coastal Africa.
  • NF (Nanofiltration): Lower-pressure membranes for selective removal of divalent ions (hardness, sulphate, colour). Used for water softening, colour removal from surface water, and partial demineralisation in Europe, Australia, and parts of Africa.
  • UF (Ultrafiltration): For turbidity, pathogen, and suspended solids removal - used as pre-treatment before RO in challenging surface water applications (Africa, South-East Asia, tropical regions) and standalone for municipal pre-treatment.
  • MBR (Membrane Bioreactor): Hollow-fibre or flat-sheet membranes integrated into biological treatment tanks for high-quality wastewater reuse. Growing demand in water-stressed regions: the Middle East, Australia, South Africa, and India.

We supply 4" and 8" elements in all standard lengths (40" primary, 20" available). Replacement elements are available for all major brands including Toray, Hydranautics, Filmtec (DuPont), LG Chem, and CSM-compatible specifications.

FRP Pressure Vessels & Side-Entry FRP Filters

FRP (fibre-reinforced plastic) pressure vessels house RO and NF membrane elements and protect them from mechanical stress during operation. Genoasis supplies:

  • Standard RO pressure vessels in 4" and 8" bore for 1–7 element capacity, rated to 300 psi (21 bar) for BWRO and 600 psi (41 bar) for SWRO service
  • Side-entry FRP filter vessels for multi-media filtration (sand, anthracite, activated carbon, AFM glass) in manual and automatic backwash configurations
  • Top-mount vessels for smaller installations and space-constrained plant rooms

FRP vessels are corrosion-resistant, lightweight compared to steel alternatives, and accepted across all major export markets - Australia (compliant with AS/NZS standards), GCC, India, and Africa.

Pumps - Centrifugal, Multistage, AODD, Borewell, Seawater

The right pump selection determines the efficiency and reliability of your water treatment plant. Genoasis supplies five main pump types for water treatment applications:

Centrifugal Pumps

Single-stage end-suction centrifugal pumps for raw water feed, permeate transfer, product water distribution, and cooling water circulation. Suitable for flows from 1 m³/hr to 500+ m³/hr. Available in stainless steel, cast iron, and bronze construction for different water quality and corrosion requirements.

Multistage High-Pressure Pumps

Vertical and horizontal multistage pumps delivering the operating pressure required for BWRO (15–20 bar) and SWRO (55–70 bar) operation. These are the primary pumps for RO plants of all sizes - from 500 L/hr small commercial systems to large-scale 500 m³/hr industrial plants.

AODD Pumps (Air-Operated Double Diaphragm)

Chemical-resistant AODD pumps for precise, pulsed chemical dosing - antiscalants, acids, caustic, biocides - and for handling aggressive or viscous liquids in chemical treatment systems. Available in PVDF, polypropylene, stainless steel, and aluminium body materials.

Submersible Borewell Pumps

Stainless steel submersible pumps for extracting groundwater from boreholes and wells at depths from 20 m to 300 m+. Essential for borewell-fed RO systems across India (Tamil Nadu, Rajasthan, Gujarat, Andhra Pradesh), parts of Africa, and Australia's rural water supply sector.

Seawater / Corrosion-Resistant Pumps

Duplex stainless steel and super-duplex pump configurations for seawater intake, SWRO feed, and brine discharge in coastal desalination plants. Chloride-resistant construction essential for the harsh conditions of Gulf seawater and coastal Australian or African installations.

Filter Cartridges & Housings

Pre-filtration before RO membranes is essential for protecting membrane elements from premature fouling. Genoasis supplies the full range of cartridge types:

  • Melt blown polypropylene (PP): 1–100 micron, the most widely used general-purpose cartridge for pre-RO filtration
  • String wound: Depth filtration for high-turbidity water, long service life, available in cotton, polypropylene, and glass fibre
  • Pleated (polyester, PTFE, nylon): Higher flow rate, lower pressure drop for the same micron rating, ideal for pharmaceutical, food, and beverage applications
  • Activated carbon block: Chlorine removal upstream of polyamide RO membranes (chlorine above 0.1 ppm permanently damages polyamide). Also reduces taste, odour, and organics in drinking water applications
  • High-flow pleated: Large-diameter cartridges for reduced change frequency in high-flow industrial systems
  • Single, twin, triple, and multi-cartridge housings in polypropylene, stainless steel, and painted steel for standard 10", 20", and 40" cartridge lengths

Sand Media & Ion Exchange Resins

Media filtration and ion exchange are the backbone of water pre-treatment and softening systems worldwide. Genoasis supplies:

Filter Media

  • Filter sand and gravel: Silica sand in 0.5–1.5 mm grades for multi-media filter beds; gravel support layers
  • Anthracite: Top-layer media in dual-media filters for improved filtration efficiency and reduced backwash frequency
  • AFM (Activated Filter Media): Activated glass media outperforming sand in turbidity removal and biostatic resistance - growing adoption in Australian municipal and aquatic centre filtration
  • Activated carbon (granular and extruded): For chlorine removal, colour reduction, and organics adsorption in pre-treatment or post-treatment applications
  • MBBR media (Moving Bed Biofilm Reactor): Polyethylene carrier media for biological wastewater treatment - high specific surface area (400–900 m²/m³) for aerobic and anoxic treatment stages in ETP and STP plants

Ion Exchange Resins

  • Strong cation exchange resins for water softening (removing hardness: Ca²⁺, Mg²⁺) and demineralisation - widely used in boiler feed water pre-treatment across India, the GCC, and industrial applications globally
  • Strong anion exchange resins for silica and anion removal in mixed-bed demineralisers for high-purity water in pharmaceutical and power generation applications
  • Mixed-bed resins for ultra-pure water production (resistivity >10 MΩ·cm) in electronics, pharma, and laboratory applications

Water Treatment Chemicals

Water treatment chemistry is what keeps an RO system running between membrane replacements. The wrong chemical selection - or no chemical dosing at all - is the primary cause of premature membrane failure in plants worldwide.

Antiscalants

Phosphonate and polymer-based antiscalants prevent calcium carbonate, calcium sulphate, silica, barium sulphate, and strontium sulphate scale from depositing on RO membranes. The correct antiscalant product and dose rate depend on your specific feed water chemistry. Typical dose rates are 2–5 ppm. Genoasis supplies antiscalants compatible with all major membrane brands and suitable for the challenging feed water chemistry found in India (high silica, high carbonate), the GCC (high-sulphate Gulf seawater), and Africa (fluoride, iron-containing groundwater).

Coagulants and Flocculants

Aluminium sulphate (alum), polyaluminium chloride (PAC), and ferric chloride coagulants for clarification and turbidity reduction in pre-treatment. Polyacrylamide flocculants for improving sludge settling in clarifiers and dissolved air flotation (DAF) units. Used in surface water treatment plants across Africa, India, and South-East Asia where raw water turbidity is a primary challenge.

Biocides and Non-Oxidising Disinfectants

DBNPA (2,2-dibromo-3-nitrilopropionamide) and isothiazolone-based biocides for controlling bacterial and algal growth (biofouling) in RO systems. Non-oxidising biocides are used specifically because polyamide membranes cannot tolerate chlorine. Critical for systems in warm-climate regions (India, Middle East, Africa, South-East Asia, tropical Australia) where biofouling is accelerated by high water temperatures.

Membrane Cleaners

Alkaline cleaners (pH 11–12) for removing biofilm, silt, and organic foulants from membrane surfaces. Acid cleaners (pH 2–3) for dissolving carbonate and metal-oxide scale. Supplied as concentrated liquids or powder formulations with full CIP procedure guidance. Essential maintenance items for any operating RO plant - particularly in regions with challenging feed water where cleaning frequency is high.

Sodium Metabisulphite (SMBS)

Used to neutralise residual chlorine upstream of polyamide RO membranes when the feed water supply contains chlorine. Essential wherever municipal water or chlorinated borewell water is the RO feed source. Dose at 3–5 ppm (2–3x the measured free chlorine concentration) and verify with an online chlorine analyser.

Wastewater Equipment - ETP and STP Plant Supplies

Growing regulatory requirements for effluent treatment and water reuse are driving demand for wastewater treatment equipment worldwide. Genoasis supplies:

Fine Bubble Diffusers

Ceramic and membrane fine bubble diffusers generating bubbles below 2 mm diameter for highly efficient oxygen transfer in aerobic biological treatment (activated sludge, MBBR, MBR processes). Superior to coarse bubble diffusers in oxygen transfer efficiency (OTE) - critical for minimising aeration energy cost, which typically represents 50–60% of ETP/STP operating cost.

Tube Settlers

Inclined PVC tube settler modules for increasing the effective settling area in clarifiers and sedimentation tanks without expanding the tank footprint. Retrofit-friendly and widely used in both new plant design and upgrading of overloaded clarifiers in India, Africa, and the Middle East.

Screw Press and Belt Press Sludge Dewatering

Screw press thickeners and dewatering units for concentrating biological sludge from ETP/STP plants - reducing sludge volume and disposal costs. Belt filter presses for higher-throughput sludge dewatering in larger municipal and industrial plants. Widely used in India and the GCC where land disposal of liquid sludge is restricted.

DAF Units (Dissolved Air Flotation)

Dissolved air flotation systems for removing suspended solids, fats, oils, and greases from industrial effluent in food processing, dairy, edible oil, and slaughterhouse wastewater - before biological treatment. Used across food processing plants in Australia, New Zealand, India, and Africa.

Water Quality Instruments & Lab Apparatus

Accurate, reliable water quality monitoring is the foundation of effective water treatment plant operation. Genoasis supplies both portable field instruments and online continuous monitoring systems:

Portable Instruments

  • TDS / conductivity meters: Handheld TDS and conductivity pens and bench meters for quick feed water and permeate quality checks
  • pH meters: Portable pH pens and bench-top pH meters with ATC (automatic temperature compensation) for dosing control and water quality monitoring
  • Dissolved oxygen (DO) meters: For monitoring aeration efficiency in biological treatment systems (ETP, STP, aquaculture)
  • Chlorine analysers: Portable colorimetric and digital chlorine meters for free and total chlorine measurement in disinfection dosing control and compliance testing
  • Turbidity meters: Portable and bench turbidity meters (NTU) for raw water characterisation and filter performance verification
  • ORP meters: Oxidation-reduction potential measurement for disinfection and redox process control

Online and Panel-Mount Instruments

  • Online TDS/conductivity transmitters for continuous RO permeate and concentrate monitoring
  • Online pH controllers with automatic chemical dosing relay outputs
  • Electromagnetic and ultrasonic flow meters for process flow measurement
  • Online turbidity sensors for filter breakthrough detection and clarifier monitoring

Disinfection Products - UV, Ozone, Chlorination

Disinfection is the final barrier against waterborne pathogens in any potable water treatment system. Genoasis supplies three core disinfection technologies:

UV Disinfection Systems

Low and medium-pressure UV reactors for flow rates from 100 L/hr (small point-of-use systems) to 500+ m³/hr (municipal scale). UV disinfection inactivates bacteria, viruses, and protozoa (including Cryptosporidium and Giardia - resistant to chlorine) without adding any chemical residual to the water. Ideal for final treatment in drinking water systems, food and beverage processing, pharmaceutical water, and swimming pool/recreational water treatment. Widely used in Australia (where Cryptosporidium compliance is mandatory), New Zealand, and increasingly in the Middle East and Africa.

Ozone Generators

Corona discharge ozone generators for water and wastewater disinfection, colour and taste/odour removal, and advanced oxidation. Ozone is a powerful oxidant (2.5x more powerful than chlorine) that leaves no harmful residual. Used in drinking water treatment, bottled water plants, swimming pools, wastewater reuse, and industrial process water. Ozone nanobubble technology (see below) further enhances ozone contact efficiency.

Sodium Hypochlorite Dosing and On-Site Generation

Liquid sodium hypochlorite dosing systems - metering pumps, day tanks, and dosing control panels - for chlorine disinfection of drinking water, wastewater effluent, and cooling water systems. On-site hypochlorite generators (electrolytic cells using salt and water) eliminate the need to transport and store concentrated chlorine chemicals - a significant safety and logistics advantage for remote sites in Africa, the Middle East, and Australia's rural regions.

Valves, Couplings & Fittings

Water treatment systems require a wide range of valves and fittings across different pressure ratings, materials, and functions. Genoasis supplies:

  • Manual ball valves: PVC, stainless steel, brass, and CPVC - for isolation and flow control in RO, filtration, and chemical dosing circuits
  • Butterfly valves: Wafer and lug-type butterfly valves in EPDM, NBR, and PTFE-lined versions for larger pipework isolation (DN50–DN600)
  • Check valves: Spring-loaded and swing check valves to prevent backflow in RO permeate lines, pump discharge, and chemical dosing points
  • Solenoid valves: Two-way and three-way electrically actuated solenoid valves for automatic RO systems, backwash control, and chemical dosing automation
  • High-pressure RO fittings: Push-fit and compression-type tubing fittings (John Guest, Parker and equivalents) for 1/4", 3/8", 1/2" tubing in compact RO skids
  • HDPE and PVC pipe fittings: Compression and threaded fittings for low-pressure water distribution and plant piping

Atmospheric Water Generators

Atmospheric water generators (AWGs) extract pure drinking water directly from ambient air humidity through a refrigeration-based condensation cycle, followed by multi-stage filtration and UV sterilisation. They produce water independent of any ground-level water source - making them transformative for:

  • Remote and off-grid communities in sub-Saharan Africa, rural India, and isolated Pacific island nations
  • High-humidity industrial and commercial sites in the GCC, South-East Asia, and coastal Africa where conventional water supply is costly or unreliable
  • Disaster relief and emergency water supply
  • Military and expeditionary applications

Genoasis supplies AWG units from 100 L/day (household and small commercial) to 5,000 L/day (community and institutional) to customers in the UAE, Saudi Arabia, Nigeria, Kenya, Ghana, India, and worldwide. The technology is particularly compelling in the GCC and coastal West Africa where ambient humidity is consistently above 50–70%.

Nanobubble Technology

Nanobubbles are ultra-fine gas bubbles with diameters below 1 micron (typically 100–300 nm). Unlike conventional bubbles, nanobubbles remain stable in water for extended periods - offering fundamentally different and superior mass transfer compared to coarse or fine bubble aeration. Genoasis supplies nanobubble generators for oxygen and ozone delivery in:

  • Wastewater treatment aeration: Dramatically improved oxygen transfer efficiency in activated sludge and MBBR systems - reducing blower energy consumption by 20–40% compared to conventional aeration
  • Aquaculture: Supersaturated dissolved oxygen levels in fish and shrimp farming tanks - improving survival rates and growth performance, particularly in intensive aquaculture in India, South-East Asia, and parts of Africa
  • Industrial cleaning and surface preparation: Ozone nanobubble systems for membrane surface cleaning, food equipment sanitation, and industrial process vessel cleaning
  • Water reuse and advanced oxidation: Ozone nanobubbles enhance micropollutant degradation in wastewater reuse plants in water-scarce regions

Pneumatic Products - Industrial Automation Components

Genoasis supplies ISO-standard pneumatic components for industrial machine builders, automation integrators, and maintenance teams worldwide:

  • ADN compact cylinders (ISO 21287): Space-saving cylinders in bore 12–100 mm for tight-space automation
  • DFM guided actuators: Twin-rod guided drives for precise linear positioning in assembly and handling systems
  • DSNU round cylinders (ISO 6432): Light-duty cylinders in bore 8–32 mm for clamping, ejecting, and locking
  • ISO 15552 piston cylinders: General-purpose cylinders in bore 32–320 mm for all high-force applications
  • 5/2, 5/3, and 3/2 directional control valves: Solenoid-operated valves in 24 VDC, 110 VAC, and 230 VAC for PLC-controlled circuits
  • FRL air preparation units: Filter-regulator-lubricator combinations for clean, regulated compressed air supply
  • Rotary actuators and pneumatic grippers: Rack-and-pinion rotary actuators for valve operation and pneumatic parallel/angular grippers for automated handling

Pneumatic products are particularly relevant in water treatment plants (for automated valve actuation), food and beverage manufacturing, pharmaceutical production, and general industrial automation across all markets Genoasis serves.
