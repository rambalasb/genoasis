Product Range

Cartridge Types & Housings

String Wound Polypropylene Cartridges

Depth-filtration cartridges with graded density wound construction for high dirt-holding capacity. Available in 1–100 micron ratings; economical and widely used in RO pre-treatment and general industrial filtration.

Melt Blown Polypropylene Cartridges

Thermally bonded melt-blown cartridges offering consistent micron ratings from 0.5 to 50 microns. No binder or adhesive — 100% polypropylene construction ensures chemical compatibility and clean filtration.

Pleated Filter Cartridges

High surface area pleated membrane cartridges in PP, PES, PVDF, and nylon for low pressure drop and long service life. Available in absolute micron ratings from 0.2 to 10 microns for critical applications.

Carbon Block Cartridges

Extruded activated carbon block cartridges for simultaneous particulate filtration and chlorine, taste, and odour removal. Essential for protecting TFC RO membranes from chlorine oxidation damage.

Polypropylene Filter Housings

Single and multi-cartridge transparent and opaque PP housings in 10" and 20" sizes. Pressure-rated to 125 psi with NSF-compliant O-ring seals; ideal for residential, commercial, and light industrial service.

Stainless Steel Multi-Cartridge Housings

SS304 and SS316L housings accommodating 1 to 48 cartridges for high-flow industrial applications. Rated up to 150 psi and 90°C for hot water and steam-sanitisable process water systems.

FAQs

Filter Cartridges — Common Questions

Technical guidance for water treatment engineers in India, UAE, Saudi Arabia, and the GCC.

What types of filter cartridges does Genoasis supply for RO pre-treatment?

Genoasis supplies string wound polypropylene (PP) cartridges, melt blown PP cartridges, pleated polyester and PTFE cartridges, and activated carbon block cartridges. We also supply stainless steel and polypropylene multi-cartridge housings. Micron ratings from 0.1 to 100 micron are available to suit pre-RO, process water, and drinking water filtration applications.

What micron rating should I use for pre-RO filter cartridges?

The standard practice for RO pre-filtration is to install a 5-micron cartridge filter as the final pre-treatment stage before the high-pressure pump and RO membranes. For higher turbidity feed water, a two-stage system using 20-micron followed by 5-micron cartridges is recommended. Our technical team can advise on the best filter configuration for your feed water quality.

What is the difference between string wound and pleated filter cartridges?

String wound cartridges are low-cost, high-dirt-holding capacity filters suited for coarser filtration duties. Pleated cartridges offer higher flow rates at lower pressure drop and are better for fine filtration (1–5 micron) where consistent particle removal is critical. For pre-RO duty, pleated cartridges provide more reliable protection for membranes due to sharper cut-off filtration.

Do you supply stainless steel filter housings for industrial applications?

Yes. Genoasis supplies stainless steel 316L multi-cartridge filter housings from 1-cartridge up to 20-cartridge configurations for industrial and high-flow applications. These are suitable for high-pressure service, elevated temperatures, chemical-resistant duties, and sanitary applications in food and pharmaceutical industries. Polypropylene housings for standard water treatment duties are also available.
